Privacy Policy

Last updated: January 17, 2026

Overview

ClipStash is designed with privacy as a core principle. We believe your clipboard data is personal and should stay that way.

TL;DR: ClipStash stores all data locally on your device. We don't collect, transmit, or have access to any of your clipboard data. Ever.

Data Collection

ClipStash does not collect any personal data. Specifically:

  • We don't collect your clipboard contents
  • We don't collect usage analytics
  • We don't collect crash reports
  • We don't collect any personally identifiable information
  • We don't use cookies or tracking technologies

Data Storage

All clipboard history and settings are stored locally on your device using Chrome's built-in storage API (chrome.storage.local). This data:

  • Never leaves your device
  • Is not synced to any cloud service
  • Is not accessible to us or any third party
  • Is deleted when you uninstall the extension

Permissions Explained

ClipStash requests the following browser permissions:

  • storage — To save your clipboard history locally
  • clipboardRead — To detect when you copy text
  • clipboardWrite — To paste clips back to your clipboard
  • activeTab — To detect the source of copied text
  • contextMenus — To add right-click menu options
  • notifications — To show optional save confirmations

Third-Party Services

ClipStash does not integrate with or send data to any third-party services, analytics platforms, or external APIs.

Open Source

ClipStash is open source. You can review the entire codebase on GitHub to verify our privacy claims.

Data Deletion

You can delete your clipboard history at any time:

  • Use the "Clear All" button in the extension
  • Go to Settings → Data Management → Clear All History
  • Uninstall the extension (removes all data)

Changes to This Policy

If we make changes to this privacy policy, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top. Significant changes will be announced in the extension's changelog.

Contact

If you have questions about this privacy policy, please open an issue on our GitHub repository.
